"From the Ashes, I Rise"

I’ve walked through nights without a moon,
Where silence sang a bitter tune.
The mirror cracked with every doubt,
Each whisper told me I’d burn out.

But in that dark, I found a spark,
A voice inside, however stark.
It trembled first, then learned to roar—
Not just to live, but to want more.

They told me “stay,” I chose to go,
They said “you can’t,” I let them know
That wounds may scar but don’t define
The strength I carry—this heart is mine.

I stitched my worth with threads of pain,
Turned every loss into a gain.
The storms that sought to break my way
Have only taught me how to stay.

I’m not who they expected me
To be—I’m fiercer, wild, and free.
I wear my past not like a chain,
But armor forged from every strain.

Empowerment is not a gift—
It’s born where broken pieces shift.
I’ve built my throne from lessons learned,
From bridges crossed, from fires burned.

So if you ask what made me whole,
It wasn’t ease—it was control
Of who I am, of when I’ll bend—
My story writes its own brave end.
